⚡ Key Team News - Round 13
Adelaide Crows - Without superstars Izak Rankine and Riley Thilthorpe. Mid-season draftee Hugo Hall-Kahan makes his AFL debut. Crows enter off the bye.
Geelong Cats - Relatively healthy list. Jack Bowes plays game 150. Coming off shock loss to Carlton that snapped a four-game streak.
Hawthorn - Jack Gunston, Nick Watson and James Sicily all firing. Tom Barrass a chance to return after three weeks with a hamstring. Conor Nash likely back after late neck withdrawal last week.
Western Bulldogs - Cody Weightman returns for first AFL game in 637 days. Bevo’s side has three wins by four points or fewer in the last four.
North Melbourne - Riding high off the bye and a stunning win over Sydney. Bunbury return after last year’s 10-point win over West Coast.
Fremantle - Ladder leaders on an 11-game winning streak with avg margin of 28 points. Finn O’Sullivan missing.
Gold Coast Suns - Coming off the bye. Welcome return to People First Stadium after two ‘home’ games in Darwin.
Brisbane Lions - Three straight losses (Geelong, GWS, Fremantle). Building injury list. Have been “easily outclassed”.
West Coast Eagles - Young guns Ginbey, McCarthy, Reid and Duursma all starring. Brandon Starcevich (ex-Brisbane) debuts for the Eagles after WAFL fitness work.
Port Adelaide - Came off the bye after losing four straight (slipped 11th to 16th). Awaiting fitness results on Kane Farrell and Jack Lukosius.
Sydney Swans - League-best 152.4% percentage. Coming off a 114-point thrashing of Richmond. Brodie Grundy rested last week, set to return.
St Kilda - Potential returns of Nasiah Wanganeen-Milera, Liam Ryan and Mitch Owens. Last out lost 52 to Hawks without a first-half goal.
Essendon - Dean Solomon takes over as interim coach. Archie May (shoulder) dislocated last week. Sam Durham and Jordan Ridley expected back from a list with 15 injured.
Carlton - Three wins on the trot under interim coach Josh Fraser (Bulldogs, Port, Cats). Patrick Cripps in red-hot form: 29/2g vs Dogs, 32/1g vs Port, 30 + match-winner vs Cats.
Collingwood - Have won the last 5 against Melbourne — streak began in the 2023 Elimination Final.
Melbourne - Steven King looking to follow the Daniher blueprint. Brought back to earth in recent fortnight after a strong stretch.
