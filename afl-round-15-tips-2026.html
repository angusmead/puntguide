Fremantle v Geelong Cats
Thursday 18 June · 7:40 PM AEST · Perth Stadium
⭐ Fremantle -15.5 $1.91
Analysis
The ladder leaders open Round 15 at home in Perth and Fremantle’s $1.44 H2H price reflects how dominant Justin Longmuir’s side has been at Perth Stadium all year. Caleb Serong and Andrew Brayshaw drive the midfield engine, Luke Jackson and Sean Darcy share the ruck and Pat Voss has been kicking match-winning bags in front of goal. Geelong arrive on the short turnaround after a Saturday MCG game, with Jeremy Cameron needing to be at his absolute best to keep this within range. The Cats have travel and venue working against them — Fremantle have not lost at Perth Stadium in winter conditions this season. -15.5 is the right number on a Fremantle side playing for top-spot consolidation.
Odds & Form
Fremantle -15.5 $1.91. The ladder leaders are $1.44 H2H — the shortest favourite price of the round outside the Crows. Cats $2.82 reflect the travel disadvantage and Geelong’s mid-season form drop. Banker line for a home blockbuster.

Gold Coast SUNS v Hawthorn
Friday 19 June · 7:40 PM AEST · People First Stadium
⭐ Hawthorn -3.5 $1.91
Analysis
Friday night football and the Hawks travel up the M1 to take on a Gold Coast side that has been competitive at home all season. Hawthorn’s $1.79 H2H price is tight at the line of 3.5 — market makers clearly see this as a close one. Mabior Chol and Jack Gunston give the Hawks their key-forward presence, while Will Day and Jai Newcombe drive a midfield that has been the most improved in the competition. The Suns counter with Matt Rowell and Touk Miller through the centre, with Ben King and Jed Walter providing the marking targets. People First Stadium has been a happy hunting ground for the home side — Hawks won’t have it all their way but their depth across the field is the difference.
Odds & Form
Hawthorn -3.5 $1.91. Hawks $1.79 the favourites despite travelling. Suns $2.07 reflect their improved home form but the gap in midfield class tips this Hawthorn’s way at a tight line.

Adelaide Crows v Melbourne
Saturday 20 June · 1:15 PM AEST · Adelaide Oval
⭐ Adelaide -18.5 $1.90
Analysis
Saturday afternoon at Adelaide Oval and the Crows are at their shortest H2H price of the season at $1.37 — that’s the market’s assessment of Matthew Nicks’ side relative to a Melbourne team that has been inconsistent away from the MCG. Riley Thilthorpe and Darcy Fogarty have been the most lethal twin-tower forward combination in the competition. Adelaide’s home dominance has been the story of the 2026 season and they roll into this with momentum from a Round 14 win. The Demons have talent — Bayley Fritsch, Christian Petracca and Max Gawn — but their interstate record has been a problem all year. -18.5 is big but the Crows’ current trajectory makes it gettable.
Odds & Form
Adelaide -18.5 $1.90. Crows the shortest fav of the day at $1.37 H2H. Demons $3.12 reflect their road-game woes. Adelaide’s home record and the Thilthorpe-Fogarty axis up front should see them cover.

⭐ GWS -16.5 $1.89
Analysis
Saturday afternoon at ENGIE Stadium and the Giants are $1.41 H2H favourites against a Carlton side that has been the form team in the bottom half. Toby Greene and Jesse Hogan lead the GWS attack, with Tom Green, Finn Callaghan and Lachie Whitfield giving the midfield genuine star power. ENGIE Stadium is a venue where GWS comfortably beat mid-table opposition and the Giants’ structure should overwhelm a Carlton side that has scored well but conceded plenty.

Collingwood v Port Adelaide
Saturday 20 June · 7:35 PM AEST · M.C.G.
⭐ Match of Round - Collingwood -13.5 $1.90
Analysis
Saturday night blockbuster at the MCG and the Pies welcome Port to their home fortress. Collingwood at $1.45 H2H reflects a stable side that has been competitive in nearly every game this year despite their ladder position. Nick Daicos and his brother Josh drive the midfield, Brody Mihocek and Dan McStay provide the key-forward marking targets and Jamie Elliott has been one of the most reliable small forwards in the competition. Port Adelaide travel south on the back of a slipping run — Zak Butters and Jason Horne-Francis remain elite midfielders, but Mitch Georgiades and the rest of the forward line have struggled for consistency. The MCG on a Saturday night under lights with a Magpie Army home crowd will be the difference — -13.5 looks the right side.
Odds & Form
Collingwood -13.5 $1.90. Pies $1.45 at the MCG. Port $2.76 reflect their slipping form and travel. Saturday night at the ‘G is one of the most reliable home advantages in football.

Richmond v North Melbourne
Sunday 21 June · 1:10 PM AEST · M.C.G.
⭐ North Melbourne -17.5 $1.89
Analysis
Sunday afternoon at the MCG and the Kangaroos arrive as $1.39 H2H favourites despite the venue being Richmond’s technical home ground. North Melbourne’s rebuild has produced one of the most exciting young midfields in the game — Harry Sheezel, George Wardlaw and Luke Davies-Uniacke drive the centre clearances and Nick Larkey gives the Roos their key-forward target. Tristan Xerri dominates the ruck. The Tigers are deep into a rebuild of their own — Tim Taranto and Tom Lynch remain quality individuals but the supporting cast has been overrun in too many games this season. North’s improvement curve and Richmond’s lack of midfield depth point to a comfortable Roos win — 17.5 is big but the form gap supports it.
Odds & Form
North Melbourne -17.5 $1.89. Roos $1.39 favourites. Tigers $3.04 reflect their rebuild struggles. North’s rising form and Richmond’s lack of midfield depth tips this to a comfortable cover.

St Kilda v Western Bulldogs
Sunday 21 June · 3:15 PM AEST · Marvel Stadium
⭐ Bulldogs +1.5 $1.89
Analysis
The closest line of the round at just 1.5 and we like the value on the Dogs. St Kilda are surprisingly $1.87 favourites at Marvel despite sitting 12th on the ladder; the Bulldogs at $1.96 are 6th and have been the more consistent side across 2026. Marcus Bontempelli still drives one of the best midfields in the competition, Aaron Naughton remains a reliable key forward and Sam Darcy has emerged as a serious key-position threat. The Saints have Jack Macrae running games through the centre and Mitchito Owens has been a revelation, but the Dogs’ ceiling is higher. Marvel Stadium is technically the Saints’ home but the Bulldogs are equally comfortable under the roof. +1.5 is value on the better side.
Odds & Form
Bulldogs +1.5 $1.89. Saints $1.87 favourites despite ladder gap. Dogs at $1.96 the value play on a 6th-placed side covering a 1.5-point line against a 12th-placed home favourite.
