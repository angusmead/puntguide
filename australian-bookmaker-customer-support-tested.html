Support Test · May 2026

We Tested Customer Support at Every
Major Australian Bookmaker

Same question. Every major bookmaker. All timed. The results reveal which ones actually want to help - and which ones make support feel like an obstacle.

Customer support at a betting site only matters until it really matters. Then it matters enormously. A withdrawal that hasn't arrived. A bet that was rejected for reasons you don't understand. An account verification query that's been sitting unanswered for three days. In those moments, the quality of customer support isn't an abstract rating - it's your money on the line.

We contacted every major Australian bookmaker's support team using the same method, the same question, and timed every interaction from first contact to resolution.

The Test Method

We asked the following question via live chat, then followed up by email: "I placed a bet yesterday and the funds haven't been settled correctly. Can you help me understand the settlement process and timeline?"

This is a real question that requires a human with actual knowledge to answer - not a scripted response about processing times. We scored each interaction on: first response time, whether the answer actually addressed the question, whether it was resolved in one interaction, and the overall quality of communication.

Testing was conducted during business hours (Tuesday, 11am AEST) and outside business hours (Saturday, 6pm AEST).

D+

Ladbrokes was the most frustrating support experience in this test. The weekday wait was 22 minutes - acceptable, but the response was clearly scripted and didn't address the specific question. The agent deflected to a generic FAQ page. When pushed for more detail, the conversation was transferred to another agent who took a further 15 minutes to respond with essentially the same non-answer. The email follow-up took 28 hours. Our Ladbrokes review notes this as one of its ongoing weaknesses.

TAB - Improving, But Not There Yet

C

TAB's support quality was inconsistent. One interaction was genuinely helpful - a knowledgeable agent who resolved the query clearly. The other was a 42-minute wait followed by a response that didn't match the question. TAB is investing in support improvements and there is real progress visible compared to prior years, but the inconsistency means you can't rely on getting the good experience.

What to Do When Support Fails You

When live chat isn't resolving your issue, the escalation path that works:

  1. Request a formal complaint number. Every licensed Australian bookmaker must have a complaints process. Ask for a complaint reference number - this changes the seriousness of the interaction immediately.
  2. Email rather than chat. Email creates a documented record and often reaches a different (more senior) team. Important account matters deserve an email thread, not a chat transcript.
  3. Reference your licence rights. Every bookmaker holds a licence from the NTRC or equivalent authority. If a dispute involves material money and support won't help, you have the right to escalate to the relevant state authority.
