How to evaluate a betting bonus

The headline bonus figure is rarely the most important number. Here's what actually determines whether a bonus has real value:

🔄
Wagering requirements
How many times you must bet the bonus before withdrawing. Lower is better. A $200 bonus with 1x turnover is far better than $500 with 10x.
🎯
Eligible markets
Some bonuses only count toward turnover on specific sports or at minimum odds. Restricted markets make bonuses harder to clear.
⏰
Expiry date
Bonuses that expire in 7 days or less are much harder to use than those with 30+ days. Short expiry windows pressure you into poor bets.
💳
Deposit requirements
Most bonuses require a minimum deposit. Check whether your preferred payment method qualifies — some methods are excluded.
📋
Bonus bet vs cash
Bonus bets and cash are very different. With bonus bets, only the winnings (not the stake) are credited. A $100 bonus bet winning at $2.00 pays $100, not $200.
🔁
Ongoing promotions
A smaller welcome offer at a bookmaker with great ongoing promos (price boosts, multi insurance, same-race refunds) is often worth more long-term.

Types of betting bonuses in Australia

Deposit match: The bookmaker matches your first deposit up to a set amount in bonus bets. E.g. deposit $100, get $100 in bonus bets.

Bonus bet on first loss: If your first bet loses, you receive a bonus bet equal to your stake (up to a limit). One of the most punter-friendly formats.

Money back as bonus: Similar to first-loss offers, but the refund comes as a bonus bet rather than cash. Wagering requirements apply before withdrawal.

Free bets: Credited to your account without a deposit requirement. Usually small in value and come with restrictions on eligible markets.

Odds boosts: Enhanced odds on a specific market for a limited time. Typically capped at a maximum profit and restricted to one per customer.

Important warnings about betting bonuses

Bonuses are not free money. Every bonus comes with conditions that limit its value. Read the full terms before depositing.

Responsible gambling: Never deposit more than you can afford to lose, even when chasing a bonus offer. Bonuses should not influence your deposit amount.

Account restrictions: Bookmakers can and do limit accounts of winning customers. A large welcome bonus from a bookmaker that restricts winners is worth less than it appears.
