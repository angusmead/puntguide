Code Differences

AFL vs NRL — why the best bookmaker differs by code

AFL and NRL are both Australian football codes, but they have meaningfully different betting landscapes. AFL has more complex player market structures (disposals, marks, tackles, clearances) that reward bookmakers with deep stat-tracking. NRL has simpler but higher-volume player markets (tries, line breaks, runs) with a strong same-game multi culture.

The same-game multi builder quality also differs between codes — some platforms handle AFL stats better, others are optimised for NRL market combinations. This is why our AFL-specific and NRL-specific picks differ slightly from each other.

If you only bet one code, go to the specific section below. If you bet both, the overall rankings represent the best balance across both.

AFL Specific

Best bookmakers specifically for AFL betting

For AFL player markets (disposals, marks, tackles, goals): Sportsbet and Dabble have the deepest coverage. Sportsbet offers the most exotic stat types; Dabble’s social feed surfaces the best player market selections from sharp bettors.

For AFL head-to-head and line odds: Ladbrokes and PointsBet are consistently the most competitive. Build a habit of checking both before placing larger head-to-head bets.

For AFL same-game multis: Dabble for AFL-specific SGMs. The social feed shows you what combinations other punters are building, which is genuinely useful for finding overlooked stat combinations.

For AFL futures (Premiership, Coleman Medal, Brownlow Medal): Sportsbet has the deepest futures market. PointsBet often has better prices on specific teams.

NRL Specific

Best bookmakers specifically for NRL betting

For NRL player markets (tries, line breaks, runs, tackles): Sportsbet has the most. PointsBet’s PointsBetting markets on NRL stats — like total tries in a match — are unique to the Australian market and worth exploring.

For NRL head-to-head and line odds: Ladbrokes and PointsBet are neck and neck. Check both. Significant pricing differences between the two occur most frequently in State of Origin, finals, and games with heavy public betting interest.

For NRL same-game multis: betr is the standout — their NRL SGM builder handles try scorer, line bet, and half/full result combinations faster and more intuitively than any other platform. Dabble is second.

For NRL season futures (Premiership, Dally M Medal, top try scorer): Sportsbet opens the most markets earliest in the season. PointsBet often has value on specific team prices, particularly for teams the market has underestimated.

Betting Tips

How to bet smarter on AFL and NRL

Line betting beats head-to-head for value. The line market forces bookmakers to set a handicap, which creates pricing inefficiencies — especially in mismatched games where casual money pushes the favourite price down. Line betting is where more sophisticated punters find edge.

Player disposal and try scorer markets reward research. Bookmakers set lines using season averages and recent form, but they often don’t fully account for matchup-specific factors — the defensive pattern of the opposition, whether the player is managing an injury, or positional changes in that round. This is where homework translates into value.

Live in-play can be more accurate than pre-game markets. Once a game is underway, the market is drawing on live data. But early in a game, before the flow is established, there can be overreactions to early score changes that create value. Many experienced punters wait 10–15 minutes before placing live bets for this reason.
