How we ranked cashout bookmakers

We assessed each bookmaker across four criteria: market range (how many bets are eligible for cashout), value (how close offers are to fair value), reliability (whether the cashout feature actually works at crunch moments) and features (partial cashout, auto cashout, live cashout).

No cashout offer is ever at fair value -- every bookmaker builds their margin into the offer. The best you can do is choose bookmakers where the feature is widely available, reliably priced and offered on the markets you actually bet. What makes a good cashout feature?

1. Market range

The best bookmakers offer cashout on almost every market -- NRL and AFL head-to-head, line bets, same-game multis, racing pre-race bets and multi bets. Weaker operators restrict cashout to only a handful of markets or only make it available on head-to-head bets.

2. Partial cashout

Partial cashout lets you lock in a percentage of your bet -- say, 50% -- while leaving the rest to run. This is a more sophisticated risk management tool than a full cashout. Only Bet365 and Sportsbet offer this consistently in Australia.

3. Pricing reliability

Some bookmakers show a cashout offer but then suspend it at the exact moment you want to accept -- usually when prices are moving quickly in a live game. The best operators (Bet365, Sportsbet, BetRight) keep their cashout feature available even when markets are volatile.

4. Fairness of the offer

Every cashout offer in Australia includes the bookmaker's margin -- this is unavoidable. But the size of the margin varies. Use our Cashout Calculator to measure the gap on your specific bet before you accept any offer.

5. Multi cashout

Cashing out a multi as individual legs settle is the most complex cashout scenario. Bet365 and Sportsbet handle this best -- each settled leg adjusts the remaining offer in real time. Smaller operators often restrict multi cashout to all-or-nothing after all legs are settled.

The cashout margin explained

When you cash out, your bookmaker is buying your position back from you. Like any business transaction, they need to make a margin. They calculate your cashout offer using their own internal odds -- which are worse than best available market price -- producing an offer that is systematically below fair value.

The formula for fair cashout is simple:

Fair cashout = (Stake × Original Odds) ÷ Current Best Price

Example: $100 @ 4.00, current best price 2.50
Fair cashout = ($100 × 4.00) ÷ 2.50 = $160.00

If your bookie offers $138, they are keeping $22 (13.75%) of your bet's true value. That is normal -- but knowing the number lets you make an informed decision.
