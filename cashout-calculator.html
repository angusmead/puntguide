When you cash out a bet, your bookie is effectively buying back your position. Like any business transaction, they need to make a margin on that trade.

The mathematically fair cashout is calculated as:

Fair cashout = (Original Stake × Original Odds) ÷ Current Odds

Example: $50 @ 4.00, current odds 2.50
Fair cashout = ($50 × 4.00) ÷ 2.50 = $80.00

Your bookie calculates their cashout offer using their internal odds — which include their margin (overround). This is typically 5-15% worse than the best available market price. The result is a cashout offer that is systematically below fair value.

This isn't illegal or even unusual — it's how bookmakers profit from the cashout feature. But most punters have no way to check the gap. That's what this calculator is for.

For each-way bets, the same logic applies to both the win and place portions separately. The place portion uses place odds derived from the win odds and the place fraction (e.g., 1/4 of win odds minus 1).

If you want to trade out a bet at true fair value, Betfair Exchange is the closest alternative — you lay your selection at the current exchange price, locking in profit or limiting loss without the bookie margin built in.
