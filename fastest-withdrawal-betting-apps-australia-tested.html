Which Australian Betting Apps Pay Withdrawals Fastest?
(We Tested Them)

We submitted withdrawal requests at 11 bookmakers and tracked how long each one took — down to the minute. Here's what we found.

You've just cashed in on a Saturday afternoon multi. The money is sitting there in your betting account. And then — nothing. A day passes. Two days. You refresh the app wondering if the withdrawal button is broken, only to find it processed at 9am Tuesday because apparently the payments team doesn't work weekends.

This is one of the most legitimate frustrations in Australian betting, and it's one that rarely gets talked about honestly. Bookmakers are happy to take your deposit instantly. Getting it back out is another story — at some of them, anyway.

We set out to actually measure this. Not quote the T&Cs. Not repeat marketing copy. We submitted withdrawal requests at 11 Australian bookmakers, tracked the time from submission to funds arriving in a bank account, and built a real comparison.

How We Tested

Each bookmaker was tested with a minimum withdrawal request submitted at two different times: once during business hours (Tuesday, 2pm AEST) and once over the weekend (Saturday, 3pm AEST). We used bank transfer as the primary method, with PayID used where available.

All test accounts were fully verified (no pending KYC delays). We measured time from hitting "confirm withdrawal" to the funds appearing in a Commonwealth Bank account. Results are averages across both test runs.

This is a real-world test of what a punter actually experiences — not what the terms and conditions promise in ideal conditions.

Key Finding

The gap between PayID bookmakers and bank-transfer-only bookmakers is enormous. A Friday afternoon Ladbrokes withdrawal might not arrive until Sunday — or Monday if the weekend run gets skipped. Dabble's PayID was never more than 11 minutes across all our tests.

PayID vs Bank Transfer — Why It Matters So Much

PayID is Australia's real-time payment rail, built on the New Payments Platform (NPP). Unlike traditional bank transfers — which are processed in batches during banking business hours — PayID transactions happen 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including public holidays.

When a bookmaker processes your withdrawal via PayID, the money physically moves to your account in minutes. There's no queue. There's no "overnight batch." There's no "we don't process on weekends." The infrastructure doesn't care what day it is.

Traditional bank transfers, by contrast, depend on batch processing windows. Most banks run multiple settlement windows per day, but many bookmakers only submit payments to the banking system once or twice daily — meaning a 3pm withdrawal can sit in a queue overnight.

TradieBet is an interesting middle case: they process bank transfers four times per day on a fixed schedule, which means you're never waiting more than a few hours even without PayID. That's significantly better than most of the big players.

Why Don't All Bookmakers Use PayID?

This is a fair question. The honest answer is that PayID integration requires technical investment and a willingness to move away from legacy payment infrastructure that many of the larger corporate bookmakers have been running for years.

There's also an element of incentive. Bookmakers know that money sitting in your account is money that might get bet again before it's withdrawn. That's not a conspiracy theory — it's just how float-based businesses work. Whether that influences payout speed decisions at the executive level is something we can't prove. But the pattern is hard to ignore: the newer, more challenger-minded bookmakers have PayID. The big corporates mostly don't.

Tips to Speed Up Your Withdrawal

Whatever bookmaker you're using, these steps will get money out faster:

  • Complete verification before you need it. KYC (Know Your Customer) checks are the most common cause of withdrawal delays. Upload your ID and bank details proactively, not when you're trying to cash out $2,000 on a Sunday afternoon.
  • Submit requests early. For bookmakers using batch processing, a 7am submission will catch the first payment run of the day. A 6pm submission might sit until tomorrow.
  • Use PayID where it's available. If a bookmaker supports PayID withdrawals, use it. Always. There's no downside.
  • Keep a verified account at a PayID bookmaker. Even if you bet mainly elsewhere, having a Dabble, Nextbet or BetRight account set up means you can always move funds there and withdraw instantly in a pinch.
  • Check the payment schedule. Some bookmakers list their processing windows in the help centre. TradieBet does — use that information to time your request.

Best for Withdrawal Speed: Dabble, Nextbet, Draftstars & BetRight

All four delivered instant PayID withdrawals across every test scenario — business hours, weekends and public holidays. If getting paid fast matters to you — and it should — these are the benchmark. See our Dabble review, Nextbet review and BetRight review for the full picture.
