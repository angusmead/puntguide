Methodology

How we actually tested this

We created verified accounts with each bookmaker, made deposits, placed bets, and then submitted identical withdrawal requests of the same amount on the same day at the same time — a Tuesday morning, to avoid weekend or public holiday delays.

We tested both PayID withdrawals and bank transfer withdrawals where both were available. We recorded the exact time the withdrawal was submitted and the exact time the funds appeared in our bank account. All times below are averages of three separate tests per bookmaker.

We did not include the initial KYC verification time in our results — all accounts were fully verified before testing began. First-time withdrawals from unverified accounts will always take longer regardless of the bookmaker.

💡 Key finding: PayID withdrawals are consistently 4–8x faster than bank transfer at every bookmaker we tested. If fast payouts matter to you, setting up PayID in your account settings is the single most impactful thing you can do — more so than choosing a specific bookmaker.

The Technology

NPP — the infrastructure behind instant payouts

The bookmakers paying you in minutes aren't doing anything magical. They're using NPP — Australia's New Payments Platform — infrastructure built specifically for real-time payments between Australian financial institutions, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

PayID, OSKO and PayTo are all platforms that run on top of NPP. When a bookmaker like Dabble, Nextbet, Draftstars or BetRight processes your withdrawal via NPP, the payment lands in your bank account almost immediately. Over 100 Australian financial institutions now accept NPP payments — if your bank supports PayID transfers, it supports instant bookmaker withdrawals.

Traditional bank transfers work differently. They rely on batch settlement windows during banking hours — meaning a 3pm withdrawal can sit in a queue overnight, or longer over a weekend. The NPP eliminates all of that.

How to set up PayID: Log into your bookmaker account, go to withdrawals, and select PayID or OSKO. Link your phone number or email address from your bank account once — all future withdrawals use it automatically.

💡 Key finding: The gap between NPP bookmakers and bank-transfer-only bookmakers is enormous. A Friday afternoon withdrawal from a bank-transfer bookmaker might not arrive until Monday. Dabble's PayID clears in minutes on the same Friday night.
Watch Out

The cancel withdrawal problem

Here's something the betting industry doesn't advertise: many Australian bookmakers offer a cancel withdrawal button — a feature that lets you pull a pending withdrawal back into your betting account before it's processed.

Picture this: you've had a big night, you're up $1,200, and you've wisely hit withdraw before you do something impulsive. But there's another match on. The money is sitting in "pending." One click and it's back in your account.

That's not a feature designed to help punters. It's friction working against you — engineered to keep money in the system at the exact moment you're most vulnerable. We're not fans of this approach at all.

Bookmakers that use NPP-based instant payments eliminate this problem entirely. Once the payment fires, the money is in your bank. There is no pending state, no cancel button, no temptation window. That's one more reason instant-payout bookmakers are the better choice for punters who take their bankroll management seriously.

We're not fans of this approach at all. Bookmakers that get your money out fast and make it irreversible are the ones we back — and it's one of the reasons instant NPP payers rank where they do on this page.

💡 What you can do: Some bookmakers — including BetNation and BetJet — allow you to disable the cancel withdrawal feature directly in your account settings. If you're punting on a site that has this feature and think it could be a problem, go into your settings and switch it off. If it's not in settings, contact support and request it be removed from your account.

Some bookmakers have refused this request in the past. If a site won't let you disable a feature designed to keep your money accessible for re-betting, that tells you something about their priorities — and in our view, it's a genuine red flag.
Platform Impact

Why the software platform matters for withdrawal speed

Withdrawal speed is partly determined by the bookmaker’s internal processes, but it is also significantly influenced by the technology platform they run on.

ApolloTech-based bookmakers consistently showed the slowest withdrawal times in our testing — often 2–5 business days even for PayID. This is a platform-level constraint, not a policy choice by the individual bookmaker. Several smaller Australian bookmakers run on ApolloTech, including some that market themselves as “fast payout” operators.

BetMakers and BetCloud-based bookmakers generally perform better on payout speed. Dabble and BetRight’s strong results are partly attributable to their payment infrastructure investment on top of solid underlying platforms.

⚠ Watch out for: Bookmakers that advertise “instant withdrawals” on their homepage but bury “processing times may vary” in their terms. If you cannot find a specific timeframe commitment on their withdrawal page, that is a warning sign. The bookmakers we rank highly are transparent about their expected processing times.
Practical Tips

How to get paid faster — regardless of bookmaker

1. Complete KYC verification before you need to withdraw. The first withdrawal from any bookmaker triggers identity verification. Do this proactively when you open the account — upload your ID and proof of address immediately. Subsequent withdrawals bypass this step entirely.

2. Use PayID. As covered above — it is always the fastest withdrawal method available. Bank transfers add 1–2 business days to any quoted timeframe.

3. Submit withdrawals on weekday mornings. Payment processing teams operate during business hours. A withdrawal submitted at 9am Tuesday processes the same day. A withdrawal submitted at 11pm Friday may not be processed until Monday.

4. Keep withdrawal amounts consistent. Large or unusual withdrawal amounts can trigger manual review, regardless of your KYC status. Consistent amounts matching your deposit pattern are processed fastest.
