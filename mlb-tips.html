How to Bet on MLB in Australia — Our Approach

MLB betting is different from Australian sports. Here's what matters for successful baseball wagering:

⚾
Starting Pitcher
The starting pitcher is the single most important factor in any MLB game. ERA, WHIP and recent form are the key metrics — a great starter on a bad team can win any game.
📊
Moneyline Value
The moneyline (match winner) is the primary MLB market. Unlike AFL/NRL, there's no spread — just pick the winner. Underdogs win roughly 45% of games in MLB, making them regular value.
🏟
Ballpark Factors
Stadium dimensions affect scoring significantly. Coors Field (Colorado) inflates runs. Oracle Park (San Francisco) suppresses them. This matters enormously for totals betting.
📅
Schedule & Fatigue
MLB plays 162 games over 6 months with minimal rest. Teams on long road trips, facing their 3rd game in 3 cities, or playing the back end of a series are often fatigued.
🌡
Weather & Wind
Outdoor stadiums are affected by wind, temperature and humidity. Wind blowing out at Wrigley Field (Chicago) can add 2–3 runs to a game total — a significant betting factor.
💰
Line Shopping
MLB odds vary more between bookmakers than any other sport. Always compare Bet365, Sportsbet and any other available bookmaker before placing — differences of 15–20% are not uncommon.

The run line is MLB's version of a handicap (line) bet. The favourite gives –1.5 runs and the underdog gets +1.5 runs. It is used as an alternative to the moneyline when the favourite's moneyline odds are too short. Backing a favourite on the –1.5 run line requires them to win by 2 or more runs.
