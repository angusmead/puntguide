How the MLB World Series Works

The MLB World Series is the annual championship series of Major League Baseball — the biggest prize in professional baseball. The regular season runs from late March to late September across 162 games, after which the top teams from each league enter the playoffs.

The playoff format involves a Wild Card round, Division Series (ALDS/NLDS), Championship Series (ALCS/NLCS) and finally the World Series — a best-of-seven series between the American League and National League champions. The team that wins four games first is crowned World Series champion.

Key betting consideration: Baseball's 162-game regular season creates significant sample sizes but the short playoff format (best-of-five and best-of-seven series) introduces enormous variance. The best regular season team does not always win the World Series — which is why value can be found throughout the market beyond the favourite.
