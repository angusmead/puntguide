Match Preview

Twenty-seven years on from their 1999 NBA Finals meeting, the San Antonio Spurs and New York Knicks reunite for Game 1 at Frost Bank Center. The Spurs lifted the Larry O’Brien Trophy back then in five games and have built a four-title dynasty in the years since. They’re hunting their sixth, and first since 2014. The Knicks haven’t played for a title in 27 years and last won one in 1973 — tonight is the first step toward ending one of basketball’s longest droughts.

San Antonio missed the playoffs last season. Preseason books had them at +6600 to win the title and they’ve barrelled through Portland, Minnesota and then a seven-game Western Conference Finals classic against the defending champion Thunder. Victor Wembanyama’s second NBA season has been a coming-of-age — healthy, finally, after an injury-plagued 2024–25, and averaging 27.3 points per game in the West Finals on his way to MVP honours. Stephon Castle calls him “the best player in the world”. Aside from Harrison Barnes (71 prior postseason games), every key Spur is making his Finals debut.

The Knicks roll in with the wind at their backs. Leon Rose’s rebuild has paid off: Jalen Brunson at the helm, Karl-Anthony Towns at the five, OG Anunoby and Mikal Bridges on the wings, Josh Hart as the connective tissue. Mike Brown replaced Tom Thibodeau after last year’s East Finals exit. The early returns are stark: New York is on an 11-game playoff winning streak (sweeps of Philadelphia and Cleveland after a 4–1 Round 1 over Atlanta), a +271 point differential through the playoffs — the highest in NBA history heading into a Finals, and an average margin of victory of 23.8 points across that 11-game stretch. The only two losses they’ve worn in the playoffs were both to Atlanta in the first round, each by a single point. Brunson was the East Finals MVP.

The series-level question is whether Knicks experience and shooting can outpace San Antonio’s defence and Wembanyama’s rim presence. The regular-season series went the Knicks’ way 2–1 with Brunson averaging 26.0 points and 7.7 assists across three meetings, including a 124–113 win in the NBA Cup Final. New York held a +7.3 rebounding edge and +5.3 made threes per game in those regular-season matchups. For Game 1, Mike Brown will lean on the Anunoby/Hart/Bridges trio to make Wemby work for every touch — nobody on the roster can credibly guard him one-on-one over a series.

For tonight specifically, the Spurs have history on their side: they are 6/6 in Game 1s of previous NBA Finals appearances. They’re also at home in front of a crowd starved for postseason basketball, and Wembanyama is the kind of player who tends to step up under the brightest lights. But 4.5 points is a generous start for a Knicks side that beat them three-from-three at Madison Square Garden in their regular-season meetings and currently has the biggest playoff scoring margin in league history.
