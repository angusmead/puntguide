Match Preview

Game 2 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off at Frost Bank Center on Friday night (US) / Saturday morning AEST with the Knicks holding a 1-0 series lead after a 105-95 Game 1 win on the same floor on Wednesday. And the scoreline doesn’t do the night justice. San Antonio led 67-54 with 5:58 left in the third quarter before the Knicks flipped the script and closed the contest on a 51-28 run over the final 18 minutes. Jalen Brunson handled the closer’s shift — 13 of his 30 points came in the fourth.

The box score gives away the rest. Karl-Anthony Towns finished with an 18-point, 12-rebound, 4-assist double-double through traffic. Wembanyama got his 26 and 12 but also coughed up six of San Antonio’s 13 turnovers — the kind of give-away count a team can’t afford against a Knicks side that finished with 20 assists to 8 turnovers themselves. New York’s reserves outscored the Spurs’ bench 28-20, with Landry Shamet adding 13 of those 28. Julian Champagnie was San Antonio’s bright spot at 5-of-10 from three in the first half but was shut out from deep in the second — a clean encapsulation of how the Knicks adjusted their perimeter defence after halftime.

The fix for Pop and the staff is identifiable. Wembanyama can’t be parking 25 feet from the basket and turning the ball over six times; expect more high-post touches, pick-and-pop sets and quicker pace to deny the Knicks’ half-court defence the chance to set. De’Aaron Fox (3-of-13 from the field, 7 points) needs the basket. The brain trust on the New York side will keep pulling Wembanyama away from the rim the way they did from the 5:58 mark of the third onwards — that’s when Brunson started getting whatever he wanted off the dribble, threes or drives to the cup with Wemby out of position to contest.

The market hasn’t panicked. Sportsbet has the Spurs at $1.44 to win Game 2, which is shorter than the $1.53 they were for the opener — a recognition of the must-win premium more than a re-evaluation of the matchup. The total has dropped three points from 217.5 to 214.5 after the slow second half. With the series shifting to Madison Square Garden for Games 3 and 4 after this, Game 2 is genuinely must-win territory for San Antonio. Only five teams in NBA Finals history have come back from 0-2 down to lift the trophy.

Our read: Spurs win Game 2 by single digits but it’s tighter than the spread suggests. The $1.44 ML is the safer side than $1.94 for the -6.5 spread. On totals, our model leans slightly Under but Rotoworld’s lean is Over — we’d let that one walk and play the props instead. Wembanyama’s last five points totals (33/20/28/22/26) make the 25+ at $1.49 a banker. And the Sportsbet flag on OG Anunoby (3+ threes in 7 of his last 8 road appearances) gives us a value spot at $2.45 with a Game 1 already 3-of-6 from beyond the arc.
