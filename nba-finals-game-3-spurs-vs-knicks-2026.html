Match Preview

Game 3 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off at Madison Square Garden on Monday night (US) / Tuesday 10:40am AEST with the Knicks holding a 2-0 series lead — both wins banked on the road at Frost Bank Center. New York walked away from San Antonio after a 105-95 Game 1 result and then squeezed past in Game 2 by a single point, 105-104, despite a stunning fourth-quarter rally from the Spurs that wiped out a 14-point deficit.

The Knicks side that returns to the Garden is the most confident in the league. They’ve won 13 straight — the second-longest playoff streak by any team in NBA history — after losing two of their first three to Atlanta to open their postseason. Jalen Brunson, the league’s ultimate closer, hasn’t even been at his best across the first two Finals games but he’s twice produced the winning points and stamped his edge on the fourth quarter. His Game 2 line was 20 on a brutal 7-of-25 from the field, but he made his free throws when it mattered and set up the closing possession that produced Wembanyama’s ill-advised pass off Stephon Castle’s back.

The supporting cast was the real story of Game 2. Karl-Anthony Towns went for 21 points, 13 rebounds and 4 assists on 8-of-12 from the field and 3-of-5 from deep — a complete performance through traffic against Wembanyama. Mikal Bridges was lethal in the second half, finishing with 20 points, 6 rebounds and 6 assists on 8-of-13 shooting and 4-of-6 from three. With the Spurs blitzing Brunson and forcing the ball out of his hands, Bridges’ mid-range and movement shooting became kryptonite — he anchored a third-quarter surge while Brunson and Towns sat. Josh Hart and Landry Shamet provide the connective glue that has made the Knicks playoff rotation as deep as any in the East.

San Antonio’s answer is Wembanyama at the centre of everything. He led all scorers in Game 2 with 29 + 9 + 4 blocks and was magnificent in the fourth, but the last possession was a back-pass that didn’t land — a kind of give-away the Spurs can’t afford against a Knicks side that’s thriving on clutch execution. The wider San Antonio response will come from the rookie Dylan Harper, whose 16 + 15 across the first two games has trended upwards and whose usage jumped from 17.9 to 20.8 between Games 1 and 2. Harper’s Game 2 included nine fourth-quarter points attacking the rim — the kind of athletic, foul-drawing creation San Antonio has been missing alongside Wemby. He doesn’t sound or play like a rookie: “It’s not the first to win two,” was his post-Game 2 line. “We can’t really hang our heads on that.”

San Antonio brings genuine pedigree — this is the team that beat the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder on the road in Game 7 of the Western Conference Finals to get here. They’re 14-5 ATS as underdogs this year and 12-4 ATS in their last 16 road games, so the ‘Spurs cover’ case has a body of work behind it. But the Knicks profile heading into Game 3 is just as compelling: 53-29 in the regular season, 30-10 SU at home and 11-2 SU as home favourites hosting Western Conference foes. They’re the right side at $1.94 on the spread.

Our read: Knicks take a 3-0 stranglehold and the path to the franchise’s first title since 1973 becomes a question of when, not if. Brunson averages 26.6 in the postseason — he’ll come through in the closing minutes. Spurs keep it within touching distance on the back of Wembanyama and Harper but the Knicks cover the -2.5 in front of their home crowd. Knicks -2.5 $1.94 is the best bet, with Wembanyama 25+ at $1.54 the banker prop and Harper 15+ at $2.18 the value play. The Over 216.5 also has a strong angle — Sportsbet’s own insights flag that each of the Spurs’ last seven road games have cleared the total line — but with two of the first two Finals games landing just inside this number, we’d treat that as the alt angle rather than a headline play.
