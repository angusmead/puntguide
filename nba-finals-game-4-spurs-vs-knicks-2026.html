Match Preview & Game 4 Tactical Breakdown

Game 4 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off at Madison Square Garden on Thursday night (US) / Thursday 12 June 10:40am AEST with the Knicks leading the series 2-1 after the Spurs stole Game 3 on New York’s floor. The tactical chess match has escalated significantly and both coaching staffs will arrive in Game 4 with specific adjustments designed to exploit what they found in Game 3.

The Knicks’ adjustments: New York was too one-dimensional in Game 3 — Brunson dominated the ball, isolated too often, and left the offence without the ball movement that makes this team dangerous. The immediate fix is Josh Hart running pick-and-roll to pull Wembanyama away from the basket and out to the perimeter, neutralising his rim protection and opening driving lanes for everyone else. The second adjustment is posting up Karl-Anthony Towns more aggressively — KAT had just 11 points in Game 3 after dominating in Games 1 and 2, and the Spurs have no answer for him in the post when the Knicks run the right action. The third shift is a commitment to blitzing Stephon Castle whenever he handles in ball-screen situations — take the ball out of his hands and force San Antonio’s role players to make decisions. Finally, Brunson is tasked to pass more and dribble less — facilitate ball movement and take what the defence gives him rather than manufacturing his own shot every possession. The assists prop is a direct product of this instruction.

The Spurs’ adjustments: After guarding KAT in Games 1 and 2, Mitch Johnson flipped the script in Game 3 — Wembanyama switched onto Josh Hart, not Towns. Pulling Wemby to the perimeter defused the KAT mismatch and made the Spurs harder to attack. San Antonio also discovered a blueprint for neutralising the Knicks: attack KAT aggressively in pick-and-roll on the other end, forcing him into uncomfortable drop coverage situations where his lack of lateral mobility is exposed. Most importantly, the Spurs collapsed aggressively on Brunson drives and took the ball out of his hands before he could get clean looks — the turnover margin was 21-7 in points off turnovers. The Spurs also slowed the pace dramatically: they held New York to just 14 transition possessions in Game 3, down from 31 in Game 1. San Antonio wants a half-court slog, not a track meet.

The outcome of Game 4 hinges on who wins the tactical battle at the point of attack. If the Knicks get Hart into P&R action and free up KAT in the post, they win comfortably. If the Spurs can again slow the pace and force Brunson into isolation against a collapsing defence, San Antonio levels the series. Our read: New York executes the adjustments at home, MSG gives them a fourth-quarter lift, and the Knicks go up 3-1 to push San Antonio to the brink. Knicks ML $1.78 is the play, backed by Wembanyama 25+ at $1.50 as the anchor prop regardless of result, and Brunson Over 5.5 assists at $1.85 as the value play on the Knicks’ ball-movement shift.
