Match Preview

Why OKC Wins Game 7 at Home

The 2026 Western Conference Finals comes down to one game at Paycom Center, and everything points to Oklahoma City. The Thunder are 6–1 at home in these playoffs — their only loss came in double overtime in Game 1 against these very Spurs. Last season, the Thunder won two Game 7s at home on the way to the title: SGA dropped 35 against Jokic and the Nuggets, then 29 with 12 assists to close out the Finals.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was dreadful in Game 6 — 6-of-18 shooting, just 15 points, a -28 in 28 minutes. But the series data tells the real story: OKC is 3–0 when SGA matches or outscores Wembanyama. The Spurs are 3–0 when Wemby outscores him. This is the most straightforward Game 7 narrative in recent memory. SGA will not shoot 6-for-18 twice in a row. The MVP who torched Jokic in a winner-take-all game is primed to bounce back at his home court with 20,000 screaming fans behind him.

Without Jalen Williams, OKC’s bench is thinner, but that simplifies the offense. Every possession runs through SGA and Holmgren. The Spurs are a brilliant young team and Wembanyama is generational — but winning on the road, in a Game 7, against the defending champions, at one of the loudest arenas in the NBA, is an enormous ask for a side that’s never been to the Finals.

Take the Thunder. ML $1.67 is the pick. If you want insurance, -3.5 at $1.94 is value given their home form.

Series Context

The Bigger Picture

This is the first conference finals Game 7 between two top-three MVP finishers since Larry Bird and Julius Erving in 1982. Whatever happens, this series will be remembered. The Thunder won Games 3, 4 and 5 before the Spurs blew them out 118–91 in San Antonio — every game won by the home team, which sets up the ultimate home-court narrative for Game 7.

The winning margin throughout the series tells you everything: margins of 19, 24, 13, 16, 17 and 27 points. No game has been decided by fewer than 13. That’s why the Over 212.5 is compelling — both offences have shown they can go big, and neither team wins by grinding a defensive battle.

Spurs fans will point to Wembanyama — 28 points, 10 rebounds, 3 blocks in just 28 minutes in Game 6. He is the best defensive basketball player alive and has completely disrupted SGA’s mid-range game. But SGA has been here before. Two Game 7s last year, two wins, two dominant performances. Home court, the crowd, the moment — it suits him.

ESPN’s staff picked this 7–7. Our money is on the Thunder.

FAQs

Who will win OKC vs Spurs Game 7?
We’re backing the OKC Thunder. Home court at Paycom Center is a massive advantage, SGA is overdue a big game after his Game 6 nightmare, and the Thunder have won both Game 7s they’ve played in the last 12 months. Our tip is Thunder ML $1.67.
Where is Game 7 being played?
Game 7 is at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City, which is OKC’s home arena. Tip-off is 8:00 PM ET on Saturday (10:00 AM AEST Sunday). It airs on NBC/Peacock in the US.

No — Jalen Williams has been ruled out with a hamstring injury. He returned in Game 6 but was clearly not fit, managing just 10 minutes (0-1 FG, 1 pt, -18). The Thunder will rely heavily on SGA, Holmgren and Hartenstein.
What is the best bet for Game 7?

Who wins the NBA Finals if OKC wins Game 7?
The Thunder will face the New York Knicks in the NBA Finals. A Thunder win would make them the first team to reach back-to-back Finals since Golden State’s dynasty run (2015–2019).
