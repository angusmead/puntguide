Full Match Analysis

This is the defining game of Detroit's season. The Pistons are 60–22, first in the East, and have home court for a reason. Their +8.1 Net Rating at home versus Cleveland's −8.2 on the road tells you everything about where this game should be won and lost.

Detroit's offence runs through two pillars: Jalen Duren inside and Cade Cunningham as the orchestrator. Duren is the best offensive rebounder in the series — averaging 3.7 per game at a 12.4% rate — and Cleveland's interior defence has been its postseason Achilles heel on the road. He will have opportunities to dominate the glass and manufacture extra possessions.

Cunningham's assist numbers at home are the most compelling part of our SGP. When he reaches 9+ dimes, the Pistons are 3–1. He ranks 3rd in potential assists this postseason (15), and in a home Game 7 with Detroit shooting more efficiently at Little Caesars, expect him to be in full orchestration mode for the biggest game of his career.

Cleveland have Donovan Mitchell (27.9 PPG) and Harden (20.4 PPG last 10), but their road struggles are deeply embedded — 1–5 away from home this postseason is not a sample size issue. The Game 6 blowout (115–94) showed Detroit have found their level. Back the Pistons on home court to seal the series.
