The Big Picture

This is a heavyweight Western Conference Finals matchup between the two best teams in the West all season. OKC finished 64-18 and San Antonio 62-20 — the first time two teams with 62 or more wins have met in the NBA Playoffs since the 1998 NBA Finals. The Spurs actually won the regular season series 3-1 and added a win over OKC in the Emirates NBA Cup semifinal, making them the only team with a winning record against Oklahoma City this season.

But regular season series mean little when De'Aaron Fox is ruled out. Fox was the Spurs' best creator against OKC's switching defence, averaging significant minutes guarding SGA. With him gone, rookie Dylan Harper — averaging 9.0 points per game against OKC this season — slides into the starting lineup for Game 1 of the Conference Finals.

Worth noting: The team that wins Game 1 of the Conference Finals has gone on to win the series 78.2% of the time in NBA history. OKC know how important this game is — and they have a massive advantage.

Why OKC -6.5

The headline story is Fox. His absence changes the Spurs' offensive equation completely. Fox was San Antonio's most reliable ball handler under pressure, and the one guard capable of matching OKC's pace on both ends. Dylan Harper is genuinely talented — his downhill aggressiveness is special — but asking a rookie to lead your offence in a road Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals is an enormous ask.

On the other side, Jalen Williams' return is huge for OKC. His 40-point Finals performance last season showed what he's capable of when the moment demands it. With SGA running the show and Williams adding a secondary scoring option, the Thunder have two genuine stars in full flight.

The defensive numbers back OKC up. They allow just 107.9 points per game, and when Stephon Castle shadowed SGA earlier this year, the reigning MVP still found ways to impact the game through the free throw line. Wembanyama was held to 18.4 ppg against this Thunder team during the regular season — the third-lowest mark against any opponent. OKC's switching, length and intensity makes life hard for everyone.

A 6.5 point spread at home, with Jalen Williams back and Fox out, feels light. The value is on OKC to cover.
