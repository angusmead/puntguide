What Happened in Game 1

Victor Wembanyama put on one of the great individual playoff performances in recent memory - 41 points in a double overtime win for the Spurs, stealing home court from the number one seed. The entire basketball world sat up and took notice. The argument that Wembanyama, at age 22, is the best player on the planet has never been stronger.

But look at what had to go right for San Antonio. Stephon Castle had 11 assists - but also 11 turnovers. Dylan Harper scored 24 points and notched seven steals but was beaten defensively late. Alex Caruso had a wild 31-point offensive game that is not sustainable. OKC forced double overtime despite all of this going wrong for them. The Thunder were right there.

⚠ Counter-Trend to Know
3-6 SU, 0-9 ATS - Home favourites coming off an overtime Game 1 loss. If OKC had won in regulation, the ATS record is much stronger. The overtime loss specifically is a concerning pattern.

The overtime counter-trend is real and worth acknowledging. But the quality of OKC's roster, their home court and the adjustments they'll make gives us enough confidence to back the Thunder. Being 0-9 ATS in that specific scenario is a small sample - and this OKC side is exceptional.

Game 2 Adjustments

OKC need to change how they use Hartenstein. Expect less of the big man and more small-ball lineups to match Wembanyama's versatility. Kenrich Williams and Jaylin Williams (who averaged 13.7 points and 8.3 rebounds vs the Spurs in the regular season) could see more time.

Dort on Wembanyama is the key defensive pairing to watch. In Game 1, Wembanyama was just 1-of-2 when Dort was guarding him directly. OKC went to that matchup late and should use it earlier and more aggressively in Game 2. Expect Dort to be physical from the first possession.

For San Antonio, Fox's expected return is significant. With Fox initiating the offence, Castle can transition to a spot-up role and get cleaner three-point looks. Fox has historically gone downhill effectively against OKC's defence, and San Antonio won the minutes with Fox on the court in regular season matchups. But OKC will be prepared for his return - he won't walk into easy looks.

Back-screens to free up corner shooters are the OKC offensive adjustment. Holmgren and Caruso in the corners, SGA with the ball - if those shots fall, the Thunder win comfortably. OKC also typically forces far more turnovers after a loss. In their last 10 post-loss games, they created more turnovers than in the previous game in 7 of those 10 matchups.
