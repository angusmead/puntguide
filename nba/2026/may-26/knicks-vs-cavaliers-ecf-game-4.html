Key Factors

1. Knicks Are 10-0 and Playing Their Best Basketball of the Season

New York have not lost a game in three weeks. Their last 10 outings average 122.2 points scored against 99.7 conceded — a +22.5 differential that ranks among the most dominant 10-game stretches in modern playoff history. They are shooting 53.8% from the field, 37.3% from three (third in the East), and averaging 27.8 assists. This is not a team stumbling into a clincher; this is a team that has been the better side for a month and a half.

2. Cleveland Has the Second-Worst ATS Record in the NBA at Home

The Cavaliers are 21-28 ATS at Rocket Arena this season, which ranks second-worst in the league. They are also 39-59 ATS overall — the second-worst mark of any team. The market consistently overprices Cleveland in their building. Even at $2.15 the moneyline, Cleveland is not the underdog price it should be. The trend is unambiguous.

3. Brunson Has Cleveland’s Number

Jalen Brunson dropped 30 in Game 3 and has been the most reliable late-clock creator of the series. Cleveland’s perimeter defence leans on Harden — not a foot-speed match for Brunson’s drives — and Mitchell at the other end. In elimination spots Brunson’s usage typically climbs another tier. Expect 28-32 points and double-digit free-throw attempts.

4. Knicks Defence Travels — And the Under Follows

Anunoby, Bridges and Hart give New York one of the most switchable wing trios in basketball, and Towns plus the Knicks’ bench has held Cleveland under their season average in every game of this series. Cleveland is 26-22 to the Under at home and the Knicks have stayed Under in three straight against the Cavs. With model projections, recent form, and matchup data all pointing the same way, the 217.5 total looks high.
