Key Factors

1. OKC's Bench Advantage Is the Structural Story of the Series

Through four games, OKC's bench has been the most decisive single factor. Caruso, McCain, Hartenstein and Wallace have produced consistently while San Antonio has leaned on Wembanyama for 40+ minutes a night with little support from the back of the rotation. Home court for Game 5 amplifies that depth advantage — OKC can play fresh wings in the third and fourth quarters when fatigue typically swings playoff games.

2. Wembanyama Fatigue Is the Quiet Variable

Wemby is averaging 41+ minutes a game in this series at age 22. His scoring stays elite (the 19, 41, 21, 26, 33 line tells you he can deliver any night) but his impact on defence and on the boards typically drops in fourth quarters when he's logged 30+ minutes already. OKC will play through pace to expose that, particularly in the second halves of games.

3. Series Tied 2-2 — Game 5 Is a Pivot Game

Historically the winner of Game 5 in a 2-2 series wins the series 80%+ of the time. Both teams know it, both teams will treat this as their biggest game of the playoffs. Home court advantage is real here and OKC are an elite home side — they win by margin when they win at Paycom, which is what makes −5.5 the right line bet, not the moneyline.

4. Castle and SGA Are Both Volume Plays

Stephon Castle has had 6+ assists in every one of his last five games. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has had 7+ assists in every one of his last five. Both are pure-playmaker roles that scale with their team's pace and ball-movement quality. In a playoff game where every possession is structured, both volumes hit easily.
