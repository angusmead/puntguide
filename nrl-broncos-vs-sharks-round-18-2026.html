Match Preview

Brisbane are facing a near-impossible task to play finals footy, needing to win at least seven of their last eight games. That task only becomes tougher this weekend given they are missing five players from the team that played the Roosters last weekend. Reece Walsh and Payne Haas are in Origin camp while Jack Gosiewski, Josiah Karapani and Aublix Tawha are also out.

Gehamat Shibasaki back on deck and starts in the centres. That means Grant Anderson plays on a wing. In a huge boost, Deine Mariner returns from a nasty leg injury - he was in terrific form until the setback. He lines up outside Kotoni Staggs on the right side. There's a chance Maguire hands out a debut jersey this weekend with forwards Luke Gale, Josh Coric and Tupou Francis named in the reserves. Gale and Coric have been impressing in QLD Cup while Francis is a teen sensation. The forwards could come into the side given the uncertainty around Xavier Willison, who the club is hoping receives an exemption from the 11-day concussion stand-down. Hayze Perham is the Broncos' fullback with Walsh out.

Cronulla lose three players to Origin duty - Briton Nikora, Addin Fonua-Blake and Blayke Brailey. Jayden Berrell starts in the No.9 jersey. Billy Burns is back in the starting side in place of Nikora - Burns enjoyed a strong run of form earlier in the season while tasked with a starting role when Nikora was sidelined by injury. Meanwhile, Craig Fitzgibbon has gone with Tom Hazelton to start in the middle for Fonua-Blake.

A gutted Broncos side (five out) vs a Sharks side that has swapped in serviceable NRL-level replacements. Cronulla's spine of Kennedy-Trindall-Hynes-Berrell is more organised than Perham-Duffy-Hunt-Paix. The Sharks should cover. Our tip: Sharks -5.5 line. Ronaldo Mulitalo anytime tryscorer $1.60.
