Match Preview

Jonah Pezet impressed in NSW Cup last week as he returned from a hamstring setback and Jason Ryles clearly took notice, naming Pezet to return to the NRL for the first time since Round 5 to replace Mitchell Moses. It is the only change for Parramatta from last week's loss to South Sydney - which saw Ryles call the Eels players out post-game for "not being good enough to even beat a park footy team". That would usually indicate major changes are on the way, but the extent of Parramatta's injury crisis limited how much Ryles could actually do.

The Pezet call-up is a big decision considering he is leaving the club at the end of the year - it would theoretically make more sense to see what the Eels have in Joash Papalii, who is off-contract beyond this season. But Ryles also needs results on the field short-term, so the hope may be that Pezet can help do that in the interim with Moses in Origin camp.

Unfortunately for Manly, Luke Brooks' season is over, but it is a big opportunity for Joey Walsh to show what he is made of as the young playmaker steps in at five-eighth for the rest of the season. It comes after Walsh extended with the club last week. Luke Keary believes there won't be too much pressure on Walsh to produce right away: "I see him just playing a really similar role to Brooks. Jamal will control the game for him."

In welcome news, Clayton Faulalo (hamstring) and Reuben Garrick (concussion) are both back and bolster a backline that is missing Origin rep Tolu Koula. Corey Waddell replaces Blues second rower Haumole Olakau'atu. Kobe Hetherington is back after missing last week's win over Melbourne while in concussion protocols and starts up front.

The mismatch is significant. Manly's returning stars strengthen an already dangerous team, while Parramatta's crisis continues. Trbojevic at fullback + Fogarty at 7 will overwhelm Pezet in his first NRL game since April. Our tip: Sea Eagles -9.5 $1.89. Tom Trbojevic anytime tryscorer $1.80.
