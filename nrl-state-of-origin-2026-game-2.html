Key Storylines

Game 1 fallout · Red card
Kalyn Ponga sent off — suspension looms for Game 2
Ponga flew into Tolu Koula with a shoulder and head contact that the referee had no hesitation in judging a send-off. The NRL judiciary will assess the charge — if he is suspended, Queensland lose not just their fullback but their most dangerous attacking weapon. Hamiso Tabuai-Fidow or even Reece Walsh would figure in the fullback conversation. Ponga’s availability is the dominant story heading into Game 2.
Queensland must win · Series on the line
The team that wins Game 2 wins the series — history says so
No team has ever won a State of Origin series after losing the first two games. Queensland are not just playing for a Game 2 win at Suncorp — they are playing for the series. That pressure at home in front of a full Cauldron crowd can work both ways: it can lift them to something extraordinary, or the weight of expectation can flatten a side. The Maroons will feel the weight of both possibilities.
NSW halves · The biggest call of Game 2 week
Can Daley possibly drop Strange after that performance?
Ethan Strange scored a long-range try in Game 1, was one of NSW’s best players in a debut Origin performance, and proved his strong running game is exactly what Daley wanted. Mitchell Moses is fit to return from his hamstring strain — but do you drop a player who was that good on debut? We don’t think so. Strange deserves to keep his jersey. If Daley does recall Moses, he’ll face a torrent of criticism. Time will tell — but this is the talking point of Game 2 week.
Tedesco · Match-winner
The Tedesco moment — one of Origin’s great tries
NSW trailed by 10 with 23 minutes to go. Strange scored to bring the margin to 4. Cleary kicked a 40/20 and then scored himself. Then Tedesco, seemingly from nowhere, flew through the air to put NSW in front. It was a try that will be replayed for decades — and it sets up the 2026 series beautifully. Queensland will be desperate to respond at Suncorp.
Queensland spine · Game 1 form
Walker, Munster and Grant were outstanding — Walker’s grubber set the tone
Queensland dominated the first 60 minutes. Walker’s short kicking game was devastating early, putting Robert Toia over in the eighth minute. Harry Grant was scheming and conniving at hooker. Munster and Walker were creative and physical. The QLD spine did nothing wrong — a moment of recklessness from Ponga and a Tedesco miracle cost them. That spine is intact and will be hungry to make amends.
Suncorp fortress
The Cauldron — a graveyard for NSW sides
Even as series underdogs, Queensland at Suncorp is a different proposition. The atmosphere generated by a sell-out Cauldron crowd with the series on the line is unlike anything else in the game. NSW will need to weather the opening 20 minutes — Queensland will throw everything at them early to silence the visitors and raise the decibel level further. The side that handles the noise wins the game.

Match Analysis

Game 1 was a masterclass in Queensland dominance for 60 minutes and NSW resilience for the final 20. The Maroons had the better of the contest for the vast majority of the night — controlling field position, taking clear advantage of NSW errors, and scoring the old-fashioned way through structure and discipline. Robert Toia scored inside eight minutes off a Walker grubber; from there Queensland looked in complete control.

What changed the game was Ponga’s red card. Reduced to 12 men, Queensland could not hold off wave after wave of blue. Strange scored a long-range try. Cleary hit a 40/20 and then scored himself. And then Tedesco, in one of the great Origin moments, flew through the air and put NSW in front. It was a loss Queensland could not have anticipated — and a win NSW were fortunate to have.

For Game 2, the Ponga situation changes everything. If he misses, Queensland must reshape their attacking spine. Hamiso Tabuai-Fidow at fullback is the most likely option — but it unsettles the outside backs and removes Queensland’s most dangerous broken-field weapon. Billy Slater has a major selection challenge in the week following Game 1.

NSW, for all their Game 1 issues — Strange’s pass off the ground, Tedesco’s early dropped ball on the tryline, Crichton’s mixed night — are favourites at Suncorp and rightfully so. The series momentum, the Ponga cloud and the returning Moses make them hard to oppose. But Suncorp with a desperate Queensland side is never easy, and the Maroons’ spine of Walker, Munster and Grant will again be at the heart of everything.
