We Tested Every Major Australian Betting App.
Here's the Verdict.

One writer. 12 apps. 4 weeks. One very patient bank account. Here's what we actually found.

The pitch was simple: open accounts at every major Australian betting app, use them across four weeks of AFL and NRL footy, and score them honestly. No sponsored rankings. No "all products are great in their own way" hedging. Just a genuine answer to which apps are actually good at their jobs.

Four weeks later, with a dozen accounts verified, twelve first bets placed and several live-bet experiences that would make a cardiologist nervous, here's what we found.

How We Scored Each App

Each app was rated 1–10 across six criteria: UX and Design, Market Depth, Live Betting, Withdrawal Speed, Odds Quality, and Customer Support. We tested on both iOS and Android where both exist. Live betting was tested during AFL and NRL primetime - the moment apps either hold up or reveal themselves.

The Standouts - App by App

Dabble - The UX Leader

Dabble's app is what the rest of the industry should be building toward. The social betting feed - where you can follow other punters' bets - sounds gimmicky until you realise it genuinely helps newer bettors understand market movements. The navigation is clean, the bet slip works without drama, and the colour palette doesn't feel like it was designed in 2011.

Market depth trails the big two (Sportsbet, PointsBet), and there are fewer exotic markets for international sport. But if you're betting AFL, NRL and racing, Dabble covers it well. PayID withdrawals in minutes sealed its top position. Read our full Dabble review for more.

Best for: anyone who values a clean, modern experience over absolute market breadth.

Sportsbet - The Market Giant

Sportsbet has the widest market range of any bookmaker we tested, full stop. AFL and NRL coverage is exhaustive - same-game multis, player props, live betting markets that update within seconds. During the Friday night footy test, Sportsbet's live market refresh was visibly faster than competitors.

The app itself is showing its age in places. Some navigation paths require more taps than they should, and the promotional content on the home screen is aggressive. Withdrawals are solid but not instant. Still the benchmark for sheer betting breadth. See our Sportsbet review.

Best for: punters who need the widest possible market range for live and prop betting.

PointsBet - Sharp Odds, Solid App

PointsBet consistently posted the most competitive lines across head-to-head and line markets in our testing period. For punters who actually care about getting a few extra cents on the dollar - which compounds meaningfully over time - PointsBet deserves serious consideration. Their PointsBetting product (where winnings scale with how right you are) is uniquely fun, though it requires careful bankroll management.

The app is functional rather than beautiful. Live betting is genuinely excellent. Support could be more responsive. Check out our PointsBet review.

Best for: odds-focused punters and anyone who wants to try PointsBetting markets.

BetRight - The Underrated One

BetRight won our customer support category by a country mile and serves up instant PayID withdrawals to boot. Every support interaction during our test period was resolved competently and without the scripted deflection that plagues the major corporates. Odds are genuinely sharp across AFL and racing. The app won't win any design awards but it works cleanly and reliably.

BetRight is the kind of bookmaker that serious punters have in their rotation precisely because most people don't know about it. See our BetRight review.

Best for: punters who value customer service and competitive odds over flashy features.

The Ones That Disappointed

Ladbrokes has a loyal user base and decent market depth, but the app feels like it's had features bolted on over the years without anyone stepping back to look at the whole thing. The racing interface is its strongest element. Sports navigation is genuinely frustrating on mobile - finding a specific AFL market involves too many taps. See our Ladbrokes review for the full breakdown.

TAB is in a category of its own - government-legacy infrastructure wrapped in a mobile app shell. Market depth is solid for racing (as you'd expect), but the app experience is the slowest and most dated of anything we tested. The withdrawal speeds were our worst result across the whole test. TAB is making genuine improvement efforts, but it's still playing catch-up.

The Verdict

There is no single "best" Australian betting app because different punters genuinely need different things. But based on four weeks of real-world use:

  • Best overall experience: Dabble
  • Best for market depth: Sportsbet
  • Best odds quality: PointsBet and BetRight (tied)
  • Fastest withdrawals: Dabble and Nextbet (tied)
  • Best customer support: BetRight
