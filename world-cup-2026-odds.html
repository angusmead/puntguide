World Cup 2026 — Analysis

Spain ($5.50) head the market as reigning world champions and European champions. La Roja's style of play — high press, technical excellence and a deep squad — makes them the most complete team at this tournament. Their price reflects genuine favouritism rather than hype.

France ($6.00) are right behind them. The French squad contains arguably the best individual talent in world football. If Mbappé and the frontline click, France are capable of beating anyone. Their price is fair value against Spain.

England ($8.00) enter as genuine contenders under a settled squad. With a strong Premier League base and experience from recent tournament campaigns, England at $8 represents solid each-way value.

Brazil ($9.00) and Argentina ($9.50) are the South American banner teams. Argentina as defending champions at $9.50 are slightly longer than Brazil — the market reflecting squad depth rather than Messi factor at this point in the tournament.

Value pick: Norway at $23.00 is interesting if they progress deep into the knockout rounds. Erling Haaland at a World Cup, with a side capable of grinding results, could be a serious dark horse at that price.

For Australian punters: The Socceroos at $501.00 are a fun speculative bet. The Each Way terms (2 places, ½ odds) mean even reaching the final would pay out on the place portion — though that remains a very long shot.
