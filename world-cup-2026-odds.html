World Cup 2026 - Analysis

France ($5.00) have moved to the top of the market and it is hard to argue with it. Mbappé is in devastating form and this French squad is arguably the most complete in the tournament — depth across every line, a world-class goalkeeper and the most dangerous forward in world football. They are the right favourite.

Spain ($6.00) are the reigning world and European champions and remain formidable. La Roja's high-press, technical style is tailor-made for knockout football. Their drift from favourite is more about France moving in than Spain falling out of form — they remain every bit as dangerous as their pre-tournament price suggested.

England ($8.00) are a settled, well-coached side. The Premier League base means no shortage of big-game experience, and at $8.00 they represent solid each-way value in a market this open.

Portugal ($9.00) are the standout value pick in the top tier. They moved in from $13.00 pre-tournament — a significant market signal. A high-quality squad built around Bruno Fernandes, Gonçalo Ramos and a defence that keeps clean sheets. Each-way at $9.00 is excellent at 2 places.

Norway ($26.00) — Erling Haaland's first World Cup. If Norway get out of their group, Haaland becomes extraordinarily dangerous as a focal point. At $26.00 each-way (paying $13.50 place), Norway are the dark horse play of the tournament.

For Australian punters: The Socceroos have moved dramatically from $501.00 to $176.00 — the market has taken notice of something early. At $176.00, an Each Way bet pays $88.50 place if Australia reach the final. Long shot, but at 2 places this is an interesting flutter for Aussie fans.
