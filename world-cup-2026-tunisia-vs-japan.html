Match Preview & Analysis

Japan have established themselves as one of the most technically gifted sides outside of the European elite. Kubo’s Real Sociedad form, Mitoma’s Brighton creativity and Endo’s Liverpool minutes give Japan a recognisable Premier League/La Liga spine. Hajime Moriyasu’s squad has been together long enough to play a sophisticated possession game.

Tunisia bring the typical North African physicality and organisation. They’ll defend in numbers, look to win second balls, and counter through Wahbi Khazri. It’s a recognisable template that has produced results at past World Cups, but the Japanese midfield quality is a tier above what Tunisia regularly contains.

The market splits Tunisia $7.00, Draw $4.00 and Japan $1.53. The Draw at $4.00 has some appeal given the way these matches can stall, but the Japanese ability to create chances eventually should be the difference. Expect Japan to dominate possession and find the breakthrough in the second half.
