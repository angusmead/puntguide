FAQs — FIFA World Cup 2026

The FIFA World Cup 2026 kicks off on Friday 12 June 2026 with the opening match — Mexico v South Africa at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City. In Australia, that kicks off at 10:00 AM AEST. The tournament runs through to Sunday 19 July 2026.
The 2026 FIFA World Cup features 48 teams for the first time in tournament history. The previous format had 32 teams. The expanded tournament uses 12 groups of 4, with the top 2 from each group plus the 8 best third-placed teams (32 total) advancing to the knockout round of 32.
Australia’s three group stage games (all AEST):

• Sun 14 Jun — Australia v Turkiye, 2:00 PM AEST, BC Place, Vancouver
• Sat 20 Jun — USA v Australia, 5:00 AM AEST, Lumen Field, Seattle
• Fri 26 Jun — Paraguay v Australia, 12:00 PM AEST, Levi’s Stadium, Santa Clara
